<p><p>The Fixed Fluoroscopy Equipment market comprises two primary types: Remote-Controlled Fluoroscopy Systems and Conventional Fluoroscopy Systems. Remote-Controlled Systems offer enhanced safety and efficiency by allowing operators to control imaging processes from a distance, reducing radiation exposure. In contrast, Conventional Fluoroscopy Systems rely on manual operation, providing real-time imaging directly from the examination room. Both types serve critical roles in medical diagnostics and intervention, yet vary in their technological advancements and operational techniques to meet diverse clinical needs.</p></p>
