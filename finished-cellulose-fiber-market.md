<p><p>The finished cellulose fiber market is primarily divided into two types: natural cellulose fibers and man-made cellulose fibers. Natural cellulose fibers, sourced from plants like cotton, flax, and hemp, emphasize sustainability and biodegradability. In contrast, man-made cellulose fibers, derived from regenerated cellulose, such as viscose and lyocell, are engineered to enhance properties like strength and moisture absorption. Both categories cater to diverse applications in textiles, paper, and non-woven industries, driven by consumer demand for eco-friendly and performance-oriented materials.</p></p>
