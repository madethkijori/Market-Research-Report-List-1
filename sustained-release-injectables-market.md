<p><p>The sustained release injectables market encompasses three main types: microparticles, implants, and gels. Microparticles are tiny particles that encapsulate active substances, allowing for gradual release into the bloodstream, enhancing drug efficacy and reducing dosing frequency. Implants are solid forms inserted under the skin, providing long-term release of medications over extended periods. Gels are semi-solid formulations that can sustain drug release through diffusion or polymer degradation, facilitating controlled delivery. Together, these types improve therapeutic outcomes and patient compliance.</p></p>
